Shall I compare thee to a summer's day? (Sonnet 18)

Shall I compare thee to a summer's day?
Thou art more temperate
Rough wind do shake the darling buds of May,
And summer's lease hath all too short a date

Sometime too hot the eye of heaven shine,
And often is his gold complextion dimmed;
And every fair from fair some time declines,

By chance, or nature's changing course, untrimmed;
But thy eternal summer shall not face,
Nor lose possession of that fair thou aw'st,
Nor shall death brag thou wand'rest in his shade,

When in eternal lines to Time thou grow'st.
So long as men can breathe, or eyes can see
So long lives this, and this gives life to thee.

All the worlds a stage

All the world's a stage,
And all the men and women merely players;
They have their exits and their entrances,
And one man in his time plays many parts,
His acts being sven ages.
